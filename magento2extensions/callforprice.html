Magento 2 Call For Price

Magento 2 call for price is a feature that allows store owners to hide product prices and replace them with a "Call for Price" button. This can be useful for a variety of reasons, such as when:

  • The price of the product changes frequently
  • The product is out of stock
  • The product is custom-made or requires a quote
  • The store owner wants to negotiate with customers on a price

To enable call for price in Magento 2, you can use a third-party extension or create a custom module. If you are using a third-party extension, simply install and configure the extension according to the instructions provided by the developer.

If you are creating a custom module, you will need to:

  1. Create a new module.
  2. Create a block that will render the "Call for Price" button.
  3. Register the block with Magento.
  4. Override the Magento price template to display the "Call for Price" button instead of the price.
  5. Enable the module.

Once call for price is enabled, customers will see a "Call for Price" button instead of the price for certain products. When a customer clicks on the button, they will be taken to a form where they can submit a request for a quote.

Store owners can then review the quote requests and respond to customers with a price. Here are some of the benefits of using call for price in Magento 2:

  • It allows store owners to negotiate with customers on a price.
  • It can help to reduce cart abandonment, as customers are more likely to add a product to their cart if they know that they can negotiate the price.
  • It can help to increase sales, as store owners can offer customers personalized discounts and promotions.
  • It can help to improve customer satisfaction, as customers appreciate the ability to negotiate a price that is fair to both parties.

If you are considering using call for price in your Magento 2 store, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Make sure that you are using a reputable extension or custom module.
  • Configure the call for price feature carefully, such as which products you want to apply it to and how you want to handle quote requests.
  • Make sure that you have a process in place for responding to quote requests promptly and professionally.
